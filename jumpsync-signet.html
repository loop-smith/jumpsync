The engraved horse is the hero mark — letterhead, saddle pad, the top of the site. It's beautiful and it dies at tab size. This is the other half: the signet. Same estate language — ring, serif, restraint — but built to survive being 16 pixels tall. Every mark below is shown at real favicon size on the right. Judge them at 16, not at 128.
Signet I

The Seal

Wax seal. Solid ink, cream letters, one hairline ring. The most legible of the four at 16 — the dark disc holds its shape against any tab colour.
Signet II

The Ring

The crest's double ring, distilled — the direct descendant of Concept III in your samples. Lighter and more "stationery" than the Seal. Honest warning: the inner hairline is the first thing to vanish at 16.
Signet III

The Rail

The Seal with two gold rails under the letters — the fence, abstracted. It's the only one that hints at the sport without needing the horse. The gold is the one warm note in the system.
Signet IV

The Brass Plate

The stall nameplate — the brass rectangle screwed to a stable door. Least conventional as a favicon (tabs expect a circle), but the most "estate" object in the set, and it's the one nobody else will have.
